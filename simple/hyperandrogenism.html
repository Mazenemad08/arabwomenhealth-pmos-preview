Hyperandrogenism is a key characteristic of PCOS/PMOS

Around 60% of women with PCOS/PMOS have elevated androgen levels, which, despite their essential roles in female health, can harm the body when present in excess. This imbalance is often linked to insulin resistance and hormonal disruptions. Hyperandrogenism may inhibit ovulation and impact a woman's appearance, leading to various symptoms.It is observed in 60 to 80 percent of women with PCOS/PMOS, with symptoms resulting from elevated levels of male hormones (androgens).

Hirsutism

is a condition in women characterized by excessive growth of dark or coarse hair in areas typically associated with male hair patterns, such as the face, chest, and back.

Hair loss

including alopecia and male-pattern baldness, can be caused by hyperandrogenism.

Skin problems

such as acne and oily skin (seborrhea), can be triggered by hyperandrogenism.

Physical changes

may occur, including alterations in body shape and a decrease in breast size.

Menstrual and reproductive health concerns

arise when elevated androgen levels disrupt normal reproductive processes, leading to irregular ovulation, missed periods, and difficulties with conception.
