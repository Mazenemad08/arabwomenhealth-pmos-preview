Why the name is changing from PCOS to PMOS

For many years, Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) was seen mainly as a condition of the ovaries. Today, we understand that it affects many aspects of a woman's health—not just reproductive health.

Many women with PCOS may face challenges related to:

  • Hormones
  • Metabolism and insulin resistance
  • Weight management
  • Skin and hair health
  • Fertility
  • Long-term health and wellbeing

For this reason, many healthcare professionals have begun using the term PMOS (Polyendocrine Metabolic Ovarian Syndrome), because it better reflects the condition's impact on the whole body—not just the ovaries.

What does this mean for me?

The condition itself has not changed, and neither have its symptoms, diagnosis, or treatment options. What has changed is our understanding of it. The term PMOS highlights the broader effects of the condition and encourages a more comprehensive, integrated approach to care.

PCOS or PMOS?

PCOS

Focuses on the ovaries and reproductive health.

PMOS

Focuses on the connection between hormones, metabolism, fertility, skin health, and overall health.

Important note: During this transition period, you may see the terms PCOS and PMOS used interchangeably. Both refer to the same condition, but PMOS reflects a fuller and more accurate understanding of its effects on a woman's health.

A new name. A broader understanding. Better care, and a more balanced state of health.
