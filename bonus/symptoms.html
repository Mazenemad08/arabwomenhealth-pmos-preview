Symptoms and complications of PCOS/PMOS?

Although the exact cause of PCOS/PMOS is unknown, the syndrome is associated with a range of symptoms that can lead to various conditions and complications.


Weight increase and insulin resistance issues

Women with PCOS/PMOS frequently experience insulin resistance, which can worsen their condition by contributing to weight gain and creating a cycle that intensifies symptoms. High insulin levels not only drive PCOS/PMOS but also intensify it by promoting further weight gain.

Irregular menstrual periods or absence of menstruation.

PCOS/PMOS-related hormonal imbalances can cause irregular or absent menstrual cycles due to disrupted ovulation and higher androgen levels.

Skin problems

Acne and pigmentation problems are often linked to PCOS/PMOS, which, while primarily related to reproductive health, can also affect various aspects of your appearance. Increased levels of androgens (male sex hormones) can contribute to these skin issues.

Hair problems

Higher levels of androgens (male sex hormones) can result in increase of hair growth on the face and body, along with baldness or hair loss.

Ovaries that are enlarged and have multiple small cystss

PCOS/PMOS is often linked to ovarian cysts, which result from hormonal imbalances and can cause pelvic discomfort. These cysts are a key factor in diagnosing PCOS/PMOS.

Reproductive health concern

PCOS/PMOS is a leading cause of infertility in women. The hormonal imbalance in PCOS/PMOS disrupts ovulation by affecting the growth and release of eggs, leading to challenges with fertilization..

Why Early Detection Matters

Many women live with PCOS/PMOS symptoms for years without realizing the real cause, which can delay diagnosis and the right support.

The earlier the condition is recognized, the greater the chance of managing symptoms and reducing the risk of long-term health complications, such as:

  • Insulin resistance
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Weight gain that is hard to manage
  • Fertility challenges
  • Cardiovascular complications
  • Long-term hormonal imbalance

Early signs that should not be ignored

  • Irregular menstrual cycles
  • Unexplained weight gain
  • Acne persisting after adolescence
  • Increased facial or body hair growth
  • Hair thinning or hair loss
  • Difficulty getting pregnant
